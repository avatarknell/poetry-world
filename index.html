POETRY

Woes of the Dead

All we ever wanted was peace,
But alive, we couldn't get it,
Our deaths weren't merciful either,
We hung from ropes of leather,
Waiting for beautiful death to grab us

Death wasn't the salvation we expected,
What a cruel mistress she was,
Our souls were strung over flames,
We wailed but who could save us?
The rest of the fuel?

We did the unthinkable,
Hell we left it behind,
refugees from death itself,
yet we couldn't seek shelter among the living,
Our bodies had been food to the beasts of wild,
No graves for scum as lowly as us,

We are the homeless,
Dead But not in the underworld,
We roam the wters and the wild,
Hoping to get even morsels of our bones,
So we may finally have a bit of home,
In the coldness of our remains.
